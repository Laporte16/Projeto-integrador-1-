What affects us

Air pollution has gradually intensified since the mid-20th century with the rise of the ambient atmosphere and the emergence of automotive industries, which release pollution into the atmosphere.
Among the main air pollutants, we can mention smoke, inhalable particles, sulfur dioxide, ozone, nitrogen dioxide and carbon monoxide.
These substances can cause serious harm to human health. Carbon monoxide, for example, decreases the blood's ability to transport air around the body. It already has the oxidizing role capable of resisting the eyes and the increase of cytotoxics, for example.
Sulfur dioxide is related to upper airway irritations, like nitrogen dioxide, the latter can also cause severe damage to the lungs. that can cause people to develop breathing problems, such as breathing difficulties; bronchial irritations such as bronchitis; fall of the immune system; neurological diseases like Alzheimer's and Parkinson's

How to avoid air pollution?

    We can avoid the damage of pollution by starting to improve the air quality inside our homes, check out the tip below

  1. Keep the house clean:Allergic and asthmatic people develop their protective attacks in dusty and moldy environments that are the perfect habitat for mites, bacteria and other harmful to health. Therefore, Take care of cleaning.
    For this, consider the possibility of investing in floors that do not accumulate dust and are easy to clean, such as floors and ceramics.

  2. Grow plants in the rooms:Nature is always collaborating with us, because plants, in addition to decorating, purify the air in the environments, consuming their plants and the bad smell. Saint George's sword, the peace lily, the bamboo, aloe vera are some well suited for internal use.

  3. use air fresheners Air purifiers are equipment that remove suspended particles and bad smells from the environment through their filters. In addition, they can work together with the air conditioning.
